# Language Rules

## 1. Prefer Direct Positive Expression

Avoid explanatory turns such as:

- "not ... but ...";
- "it should not be understood as ...";
- "from a certain perspective";
- repeated explanation of why the report is not an approval document.

Preferred:

> This report serves as technical support material and focuses on impact range, risk conditions and management requirements after project implementation.

## 2. Replace Vague Praise With Technical Content

Use vague praise only when immediately followed by object, evidence, and condition.

Weak words:

- important significance;
- strong support;
- scientific and reasonable;
- feasible;
- significantly improved;
- systematic and comprehensive;
- further strengthen;
- continuously improve;
- overall controllable;
- limited impact.

Preferred pattern:

> The study clarifies concentration changes at sensitive sections, main risk scenarios and monitoring priorities after project implementation, which can support operation scheduling and follow-up monitoring plan preparation.

## 3. Avoid Absolute Promises

Use cautious technical judgement:

- "has acceptable conditions" instead of "has no impact";
- "has control conditions after implementation of..." instead of "no risk";
- "can reduce related risk" instead of "fully solve";
- "still needs follow-up monitoring verification" when uncertainty remains;
- "can be used as a preferred direction" instead of "the best plan".

## 4. Be Concrete About Data

When available, write:

- sample size and monitoring frequency;
- mean, median, maximum, minimum, range, or percentile;
- year/month/season and statistical period;
- location, section, receptor, or management unit;
- standard name and class limit;
- model scenario, input condition, and uncertainty boundary.

Avoid:

> Water quality has improved in recent years.

Prefer:

> From 2021 to 2024, annual average COD concentration at the main control section decreased overall. However, short-term high values still occurred in several rainy-season months, indicating that stable compliance still depends on rainfall-period control and follow-up monitoring.

## 5. Write Measures as Executable Requirements

Measures should specify as many of the following as the material allows:

- object;
- measure;
- timing or frequency;
- trigger condition;
- responsible direction;
- required record or output;
- how monitoring results will be used.

Weak:

> Strengthen monitoring management.

Preferred:

> During the initial operation period, conduct follow-up monitoring at the downstream near-field area, downstream control section and sensitive receptors. Focus on COD, ammonia nitrogen, total phosphorus and flow changes. Monitoring results should support operation scheduling, abnormal-condition response and subsequent assessment.
