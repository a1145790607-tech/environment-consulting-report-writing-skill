# Review Checklist

## Logic

- [ ] Does the section answer a clear technical or management question?
- [ ] Are the analysis object and project boundary clear?
- [ ] Does the text present evidence before judgement?
- [ ] Are background materials connected to the conclusion?
- [ ] Are same-level subsections written with consistent structure?
- [ ] Are later conclusions supported by earlier materials?

## Environmental Consulting Substance

- [ ] Are applicable laws, standards, plans, EIA documents, design documents, or approvals identified by full name/year/document number when available?
- [ ] Are receptors, pathways, indicators, scenarios, and evaluation criteria explicit?
- [ ] Are domestic standards or management requirements explained before foreign references are used?
- [ ] Are impact source, affected area, sensitive object, and control condition clear?
- [ ] Are uncertainty, assumptions, or follow-up verification needs stated where needed?

## Data And Comparison

- [ ] Are units, statistical caliber, time range, data source, and spatial boundary consistent?
- [ ] Are multiple years, sections, scenarios, indicators, alternatives, or models compared under the same criteria?
- [ ] Do figures and tables explain trend, difference, abnormal value, and management implication instead of repeating numbers only?
- [ ] If indicators point in different directions, is the primary judgement criterion explained?

## Conclusion And Measures

- [ ] Does each conclusion correspond to evidence?
- [ ] Does the conclusion avoid overcommitment such as "no impact", "ensure compliance", or "no risk"?
- [ ] Are mitigation, monitoring, emergency, operation, or management measures specific and executable?
- [ ] Does the section summary close the argument and connect to follow-up work?

## Language

- [ ] Are vague phrases replaced with objects, indicators, evidence, and conditions?
- [ ] Are long sentences split where they mix fact, analysis, judgement, and measure?
- [ ] Is the tone formal, concise, and suitable for Chinese environmental consulting reports?
